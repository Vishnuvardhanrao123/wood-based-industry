The Types of Teak Woods


Balharshah Teak,India

Balharshah Teak Wood, also known as Ballarshah Teak, is renowned for its high quality and durability. Here are some key points about it: Color and Appearance: It has a yellow tone that gradually turns brown over time. The wood features excellent black grains, giving it a distinctive look1. Strength and Durability: Known for its hardness and resistance to degradation, making it ideal for various applications1.Uses: Commonly used in furniture, flooring, and construction due to its strength and aesthetic appeal1. Maintenance: Requires minimal maintenance due to its natural resistance to pests and decay1.


Kinvat Teak,India

In terms of quality, Indian teak is considered to be a high-quality wood that is both durable and strong. It has a high natural oil content, which makes it resistant to moisture, rot, and insect damage. Indian teak is also known for its stability and resistance to warping or cracking.

Teak is a large deciduous tree up to 40 m (131 ft) tall with grey to greyish-brown branches, known for its high-quality wood. Its leaves are ovate-elliptic to ovate, 15–45 cm (5.9–17.7 in) long by 8–23 cm (3.1–9.1 in) wide, and are held on robust petioles which are 2–4 cm (0.8–1.6 in) long. Leaf margins are entire.
